About Me + Scholarly Interests

I am an instruction librarian at Manhattan College, and am working toward an MA in Liberal Studies with a concentration in Digital Humanities at the CUNY Graduate Center.

My scholarly interests include exploring the intersection between information literacy instruction and critical pedagogy, and how that affects in person and online info lit instruction. Ad an instruction librarian who teaches primarily one-shot lessons, I strive to lessen student alienation and increase participation through.........

Due to the interdisciplinary nature of my graduate program, I am able to take classes in many disciplines, allowing me experience conducting research and writing for different areas. I've taken courses in English (feminist memoir and biography, NYC narratives through literature and film), Sociology (subcultures of resistance), Art History (early Islamic art and architecture), and of course in the Digital Humanities.

Before I went to library school I was a pastry chef. I am a quilter and a triathlete.
