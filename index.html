About Me + Scholarly Interests

I am an instruction librarian at Manhattan College's O'Malley Library, and am working toward an MA in Liberal Studies with a concentration in Digital Humanities at the CUNY Graduate Center.

My scholarly interests include exploring the intersection of information literacy instruction and critical pedagogy, and how that affects in-person and online info lit instruction. As an instruction librarian who teaches primarily one-shot lessons, I strive to lessen student alienation and increase participation. I like to change things up and I’m not afraid of failure.

Due to the nature of my graduate program, I am able to take classes in many disciplines, allowing me experience conducting research and writing for different areas. I've taken courses in English, Sociology, Art History, and of course in the Digital Humanities.

Before I went to library school I was a pastry chef. I am a quilter, a triathlete, and a cat owner.
